Alexandre V. Jamet, PhD.

Enjoying coffee in a café in Oslo, Norway.

I am Alexandre V. Jamet, a postdoctoral researcher with the SONAR group at the Barcelona Supercomputing Center. I earned my PhD in Computer Architecture through a joint program between BSC and the Universitat Politècnica de Catalunya (UPC).

My research focuses on the microarchitecture of general-purpose processors, with an emphasis on memory systems, data movement, and performance bottlenecks. I have worked extensively on cache hierarchies, prefetching mechanisms, and AI-inspired predictors, contributing to publications at top-tier venues such as HPCA, IPDPS, and IISWC. My work explores how learning-based techniques can be leveraged to improve system efficiency and adaptivity in modern architectures.

More broadly, I am interested in rethinking the fundamental design principles of high-performance CPUs. Rather than incremental improvements, I aim to contribute to architecture-level shifts that better align hardware design with the realities of emerging workloads, particularly at the intersection of systems and machine learning.

Beyond academia, I have collaborated with industry partners and worked on applied systems projects, including embedded systems and communication infrastructures. This includes developing a GSM–LoRa gateway prototype and contributing to feasibility studies for real-world engineering systems.

Technically, I work across the full stack of performance engineering—from low-level programming (C/C++, assembly) and microarchitectural simulation to tooling (Python, GDB/LLDB) and parallel programming (OpenMP). I am particularly interested in building fast, practical research infrastructure that bridges the gap between architectural ideas and real-world evaluation.

Outside of research, I train daily as an amateur powerlifter. Earlier in my career, I was a member of the French National Finswimming Team for seven years, competing internationally and earning multiple medals at major championships.

  2. HPCA
    A Two Level Neural Approach Combining Off-Chip Prediction with Adaptive Prefetch Filtering
    Alexandre Valentin Jamet, Georgios Vavouliotis, Daniel A. Jiménez, and 2 more authors
    In 2024 IEEE International Symposium on High-Performance Computer Architecture (HPCA), Mar 2024
    Abs DOI arXiv PDF Code Poster

    To alleviate the performance and energy overheads of contemporary applications with large data footprints, we propose the Two Level Perceptron (TLP) predictor, a neural mechanism that effectively combines predicting whether an access will be off-chip with adaptive prefetch filtering at the first-level data cache (L1D). TLP is composed of two connected microarchitectural perceptron predictors, named First Level Predictor (FLP) and Second Level Predictor (SLP). FLP performs accurate off-chip prediction by using several program features based on virtual addresses and a novel selective delay component. The novelty of SLP relies on leveraging off-chip prediction to drive L1D prefetch filtering by using physical addresses and the FLP prediction as features. TLP constitutes the first hardware proposal targeting both off-chip prediction and prefetch filtering using a multilevel perceptron hardware approach. TLP only requires 7KB of storage. To demonstrate the benefits of TLP we compare its performance with state-of-the-art approaches using off-chip prediction and prefetch filtering on a wide range of single-core and multi-core workloads. Our experiments show that TLP reduces the average DRAM transactions by 30.7% and 17.7%, as compared to a baseline using state-of-the-art cache prefetchers but no off-chip prediction mechanism, across the single-core and multi-core workloads, respectively, while recent work significantly increases DRAM transactions. As a result, TLP achieves geometric mean performance speedups of 6.2% and 11.8% across single-core and multi-core workloads, respectively. In addition, our evaluation demonstrates that TLP is effective independently of the L1D prefetching logic.
