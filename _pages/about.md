I am **Alexandre V. Jamet**, a postdoctoral researcher with the SONAR group at the [Barcelona Supercomputing Center](https://bsc.es). I earned my PhD in Computer Architecture through a joint program between BSC and the [Universitat Politècnica de Catalunya (UPC)](https://www.upc.edu).

My research focuses on the microarchitecture of general-purpose processors, with an emphasis on memory systems, data movement, and performance bottlenecks. I have worked extensively on cache hierarchies, prefetching mechanisms, and AI-inspired predictors, contributing to publications at top-tier venues such as HPCA, IPDPS, and IISWC. My work explores how learning-based techniques can be leveraged to improve system efficiency and adaptivity in modern architectures.

More broadly, I am interested in rethinking the fundamental design principles of high-performance CPUs. Rather than incremental improvements, I aim to contribute to architecture-level shifts that better align hardware design with the realities of emerging workloads, particularly at the intersection of systems and machine learning.

Beyond academia, I have collaborated with industry partners and worked on applied systems projects, including embedded systems and communication infrastructures. This includes developing a GSM–LoRa gateway prototype and contributing to feasibility studies for real-world engineering systems.

Technically, I work across the full stack of performance engineering—from low-level programming (C/C++, assembly) and microarchitectural simulation to tooling (Python, GDB/LLDB) and parallel programming (OpenMP). I am particularly interested in building fast, practical research infrastructure that bridges the gap between architectural ideas and real-world evaluation.

Outside of research, I train daily as an amateur powerlifter. Earlier in my career, I was a member of the French National Finswimming Team for seven years, competing internationally and earning multiple medals at major championships.
